When should do I do my homework?

Many people tend to say "get it over with," and this likely applies to doing homework. If you do your work before leisure activities, such as playing video games, then you will
not have to worry about homework while doing said activity and, as a result, you will be less stressed. By getting homework done first,
it allows you to participate in other activities without the threat of homework hanging over your head. However, this does not mean do assignments right after school. Try to take at least 40 minutes off any work (watch TV,
go outside for an hour, take a nap) and then begin homework. This will allow you to re-energize and focus better on the task at hand.

What if I have tests the next day?


If you have tests to study for, it is recommended that you study right after that 40 minute break after school,
as it is better to study before doing homework. If you do homework before studying, then you will get tired and
lose focus while studying which may lead you to retain less information and as a result, perform poorly on your tests.
Time yourself well, and make sure to finish all studying and homework as efficiently as possible.

Extracurriculars

A huge aspect of college applications is your extracurricular activities, or things that you do outside of school.
These are valued very highly by colleges as it gives you an opportunity to show them what type of person you are and what you can do besides get good grades.
Important extracurriculars to include on your application include internships, leadership activities, work experiences, and club/team memberships.
By including these, you can greatly increase your chances of getting
into the college by showing them that you are more than your grades and are involved in other things that could be beneficial in college or even after it.


Help For High School Students


Welcome to HelpHighSchool

This website provides help to high school students who are struggling with college applications and sat testing by offering a multitude of resources.


Application Fee

The application fee will be around $30-$60 for applications from U.S. Residents but it may be higher for international students.
If you cannot afford the fee than colleges will give you a fee waiver depending on your situation.

Transcript (Grades)

You must request an official transcript from your high school to send to the colleges you are applying to.
The transcript has your grades and GPA on it which are one of the most important parts of the application.
If this requirement is not met than your application will not be be considered.

SAT and ACT Scores

Whenever you take your SAT or ACT, you have the option to send the scores to the college when they are available.
However, this is not recommended because if you're desired score is not achieved than you would not want colleges to see that score.
So it is recommended that you do not send sat or act scores right as they are available after scoring.
Also, it is recommended that you take no more than 3 tests because colleges will not like to see a good score after 10 tries.
Check out the SAT RESOURCES tab for some helpful resources.


SAT Test Optional

With the current pandemic, many colleges have decided to go test-optional.
What this means is that they will not penalize you for not including an ACT or SAT score on your application.
However, the score may possibly provide benefits to your application.

Letters of Recommendation

Many colleges require that you have two to three teacher recommendations.
These recommendations. mostly, include 1 core-subject (math, science, History, English) and another is usually one from an elective teacher.
An additional recommendation letter can be from any teacher or it can from a member of the community.
The one thing that should be made sure before asking your teachers for recommendation is: Do they know you well and do they have a good impression of you.
Since you have no idea what these teachers will say about you (sealed-envelopes, you have no access to the letters), you want to make sure
this teacher can speak only the best of you. Also, make sure you ask politely. Try to give them as much information about
you outside of you school life (awards, extra-curriculars, etc.).
Make sure you ask the teacher two months in advance of the Application deadline to ensure the letters are sent on time.

Writing Prompts

The writing portion of your college application is the most time-consuming.
This is because the writing portion usually includes the personal statement and a supplemental essay (if you want to get into a specific school within the college).
The personal statement is your answer to an essay prompt. This is asked for because it gives the college admissions officers a better
insight into who you are outside of the school environment. To some extent, this portion has a heavier weight than grades or sat score.
Many people have gotten into prestigious schools with low grades and a low sat scores, but with an inspiring essay.
